# Product management on the AI exponential

**Source:** https://claude.com/blog/product-management-on-the-ai-exponential  
**Published:** March 19, 2026  
**Categories:** Claude Code

## Introduction

Claude Code's Head of Product Cat Wu examines how product teams are adapting their workflows in response to rapidly advancing AI capabilities. The traditional product management approach assumes technological constraints remain stable throughout a project lifecycle. Exponentially improving models fundamentally change this assumption.

## New Product Management Workflow

Cat Wu's division of labor across three products:
- **Claude.ai**: thought partner for strategic thinking and problem-solving
- **Claude Code**: prototyping, evals, and scripts
- **Cowork**: administrative tasks, documentation, and knowledge work

## Four Key Shifts for the AI Era

### 1. Plan in Short Sprints
Encourage "side quests"—self-directed afternoon experiments testing capabilities previously assumed impossible. Popular features like Claude Code on Desktop and todo lists emerged through this exploratory process.

### 2. Demos and Evals Over Documentation
Prototype-first thinking replaces documentation-heavy approaches. Teams share working demos rather than lengthy specifications.

### 3. Revisit Features with New Models
Each model release presents an opportunity to reassess existing products. The integration of Claude Code with Chrome exemplifies this—users were manually switching between tools, suggesting built-in integration would be valuable.

### 4. Do the Simple Thing
Complex implementations designed to compensate for model limitations become technical debt when capabilities improve. Simpler code adapts more easily when new models arrive.

## Key Metrics

METR research indicates Opus 4.6 can complete software tasks taking humans approximately 12 hours—a roughly 41x improvement from Sonnet 3.5's 21-minute baseline in just 16 months.
