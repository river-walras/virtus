# Building AI agents for financial services

**Source:** https://claude.com/blog/building-ai-agents-in-financial-services  
**Published:** October 30, 2025  
**Categories:** Agents, Claude Platform, Financial services

## Overview

AI agents in financial services move beyond pilots to deliver concrete business value by autonomously managing business processes with human oversight.

## Real-World Results

**Customer Service**: Multilingual virtual assistants handle hundreds of millions of interactions annually. Intuit TurboTax's AI assistant powered by Claude achieved higher customer ratings than non-Claude experiences in the previous tax season.

**Fraud Detection**: A single team member can effectively supervise more than 20 AI agents in financial crime-detection workflows. Brex uses Claude to review 100% of transactions, proactively grouping related expenses and flagging policy concerns.

**Team Empowerment**: Block's internal AI agent reached 4,000 active users out of 10,000 employees across 15 different job profiles. Adoption doubled in one month.

## Unique Challenges

**Legacy Infrastructure**: Core banking systems use different protocols, data formats, and security models. Build custom connectors via APIs or MCP approaches, or implement middleware.

**Regulatory Complexity**: SOC 2, PCI DSS, multi-regulator requirements (SEC, FDIC, state banking). Build observability and traceability from day one.

**Real-Time Risk Management**: Requires transparent reasoning, clear escalation pathways, override capabilities, and fail-safe defaults prioritizing customer protection.

## Practical Deployment Strategies

1. **Start simple**: Flagging unusual transaction patterns, monitoring compliance deadlines, automating document classification
2. **Build reusable foundations**: Document processing capabilities that serve multiple departments
3. **Earn trust**: Be transparent when customers interact with AI vs. humans; frame AI as enhancement not replacement
4. **Advance to complex use cases**: Build comprehensive audit trails, real-time monitoring, escalation protocols
